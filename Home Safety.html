I want to share 5 easy ways on how to preve nt a fire as part of National Fire Prevention Month. Bureau of Fire Protection Organizations say that home fires are the single most common disaster across the nation. By following these 5 simple fire safety tips, you can prevent a disaster from occuring.

how to prevent fire
Blow out candles before leaving a room or going to bed. Don't forget to blow out candles when you leave the room or go to bed. Candles may be pretty to look at but they are a cause of home fires â€” and home fire deaths. Remember, a candle is an open flame, which means that it can easily ignite anything that can burn.
When cooking, keep towels, pot holders and curtains away from flames. Don't leave towels, sponges, pot holders, or paper and plastic products near the stove or any heating or hot appliances. Even curtains close to the stove should be removed or tied back. Preventing grease build-up on your stovetop, exhaust fan and in your ovens, including microwaves can also prevent fires.
Keep matches,lighters and other ignitable substances in a secured location out of the reach of children, and only use lighters with child-resistant features. Keep all matches and lighters out of reach in a high cabinet, preferably a locked one. Do not leave young children unattended. It only takes a few seconds for a fire to start and burn out of control. Don't leave children under 12 alone and don't leave them in charge of younger children.
Keep candles at least 12 inches away from anything that can burn such as bedding, curtains or clothing. Remember that lit candles are fire. Keep them at least 12 inches from anything that can burn, such as curtains, bedding, mattresses, paper, books, flammable decorations, clothing, and upholstered furniture.Always extinguish candles before leaving the room or going to bed.
Always check the kitchen before going to bed or leaving the home to make sure all stoves, ovens, and small appliances are turned off. Clean and safe kitchens lower food risks and accidents. By taking the necessary precautions, you can reduce or eliminate burns, fires, falls, cuts, electrical shocks, and poisonings in your kitchen. Any substance in food that can cause illness or injury is called a hazard.
